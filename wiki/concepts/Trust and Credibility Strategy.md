## Definition
How Essential handles user skepticism about a non-coder's AI detection app: position as a premium bridge to enterprise APIs, not a homemade detector. Transparency about the stack becomes a trust asset rather than a liability.

## The Cocktail Bar Framing
"I didn't distill the vodka or grow the limes. I combined premium, trusted ingredients into a convenient, beautiful experience."

Applied to Essential: "An easy mobile wrapper that connects everyday users to the world's most accurate enterprise AI-detection networks."

## Three Trust Pillars

### 1. Don't Claim the AI
Never say "I invented this detector." Say "I built the bridge to enterprise-grade detection infrastructure."

### 2. Borrow API Credibility
When users ask "Why should I trust your app?", point to [[Sightengine]] or [[Hive Moderation]] — multi-million dollar companies whose models detect AI in millions of images daily. The app inherits their credibility.

### 3. Transparency as Marketing Hook
Reddit / X quote: *"I have zero coding skills, which is exactly why I didn't try to build my own AI model. I used Claude to plug directly into enterprise APIs like Sightengine. I handle design and convenience; they handle the data science."*

This level of honesty increases trust — it shows the founder understands their limits and chose a professional route.

## Trust Section on Landing Page
Prompt for Claude: "Create a 'Trust & Reliability' section. Include: secure SSL encryption, no permanent user data storage, processed through enterprise-grade AI detection infrastructure, 99% uptime guarantee."
