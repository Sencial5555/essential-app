## Definition
Essential's go-to-market strategy: the founder as the authentic protagonist, launching across four social platforms, leveraging the deepfake/AI misinformation news wave, and using transparency as a trust weapon.

## The Core Narrative: Zero-Skills Underdog
"I have zero coding skills, but I got so sick of fake AI videos on my timeline that I forced Claude to help me build a tool to spot them."

Never pretend to be a customer who "found" the app. Reddit and TikTok audiences have a high BS detector.

## Platform Playbook

| Platform | Angle | Hook |
|----------|-------|------|
| TikTok | Zero-Skills Underdog | Day N of building an AI detector with zero coding skills |
| Reddit | Feedback & Roast Me | Story post in r/SideProject asking for community feedback |
| X / Twitter | Build in Public | Thread: "How I built this in 48 hours with $0 coding experience" |
| LinkedIn | Future of Work case study | "You no longer need a CS degree to launch software" |

## Timing
Deep-fake, fake news, and AI misinformation are peak-concern topics. The market is actively searching for tools like Essential. Launch during a viral AI-controversy news cycle for maximum organic reach.

## Content Production
Claude can write all posts, scripts, and threads. Prompt: "Act as a native TikTok creator, write a 30-second script demonstrating my AI-detection app, casual and slightly humorous, focus on the non-coder hook."
