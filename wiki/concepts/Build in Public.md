## Definition
A marketing approach where the founder documents the project journey openly on social media — sharing real prompts, mistakes, costs, and milestones — creating an audience that roots for and eventually uses the product.

## Why It Works for Essential
The "Non-coder builds AI detector using Claude" story is inherently compelling. Documenting it creates a narrative that tech blogs, TikTok viewers, and Reddit communities share organically. The founder's lack of coding skills is the hook, not the weakness.

## Platform-Specific Tactics

**X / Twitter:** Thread format. Share: Claude conversation screenshots, early ugly designs, revenue milestones, exact prompts used. Hook: "How I built a mobile AI-detection app in 48 hours with $0 coding experience."

**TikTok:** Screen recording. Show: viral AI video frame → upload to Essential → meter ticking to "98% AI." Hook: "Day N of building this app with zero coding skills. Here's what it cost me today..."

## Content Claude Can Write
"Act as a native TikTok creator. Write a 30-second video script demonstrating my AI-detection app. Casual, slightly humorous. Focus on the hook: a non-coder built this out of frustration with deepfakes."
