Here is the exact breakdown of why you don't need to download them and how they actually get into your project:

**1. HTML ****&**** CSS (****Built**** ****into**** ****every**** ****computer****)**

You cannot download HTML or CSS because they aren't programs—they are just **languages**.

- Every web browser in the world (Google Chrome, Safari, Microsoft Edge) is already born with the ability to read HTML and CSS.

- To "use" them, Claude will simply create a normal text file inside Cursor and name it index.html. The moment you type text into that file, you are using HTML.

**2. ****Tailwind**** CSS (The ****Cloud**** ****Delivery****)**

You don't download Tailwind onto your hard drive either. Instead, Claude will put a single, special link at the top of your HTML file (called a CDN link).

- When a user opens **Essential**, their web browser reads that link and instantly grabs Tailwind’s styling rules from the cloud in a fraction of a second. It requires zero setup from you.

**3. The ****Database**** (****Lives**** ****on**** ****the**** Internet)**

In the old days of programming, you did have to download massive, heavy database software to your computer. In 2026, we don't do that anymore.

- Your database will live entirely in the cloud on a secure, free platform (like **Supabase** or **Firebase**).

- You will just sign up for a free account on their website, and **Claude ****Code** will write a few lines of code that tell your app how to send data to that website over the internet. Your computer stays completely clean.
